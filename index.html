The contemporary breed of Sphynx cat is distinct from the Russian hairless cat breeds, like Peterbald and Donskoy.
Although hairless cats have been reported throughout history, breeders in Europe have been developing the Sphynx breed since the early 1960s.

Hairlessness in cats is a naturally occurring genetic mutation,
and the Sphynx was developed through selective breeding of these animals, starting in the 1960s.

Sphynx are known for their extroverted behavior. They display a high level of energy, intelligence, curiosity and affection for their owners. They are one of the more dog-like breeds of cats, frequently greeting their owners at the door and friendly when meeting strangers
